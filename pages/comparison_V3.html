iPhone 17 Pro Max

The iPhone 17 Pro Max represents a milestone in thermal architecture and processing capabilities. Apple's transition to a 2nm manufacturing process with the A19 Pro chipset allows for unprecedented performance scaling while consuming up to 30% less power during high-compute operations. This allows the internal hardware to run cooler for longer periods.

Figure 1: Internal A19 Pro schematic showing integration with the graphene chamber for efficient thermal dissipation.

Another prominent enhancement is the chassis build. By utilizing Grade 5 Sandblasted Titanium, the device reduces weight by another 12 grams compared to standard steel alloys while enhancing scratch resistance. The 6.9-inch display is framed by ultra-thin borders that drop display bezels down to 1.15mm, offering the highest screen-to-body ratio in an iPhone yet.

Figure 2: Edge-to-edge bezel detail highlighting the 1.15mm Super Retina display borders.

Finally, the optics segment introduces a redesigned tetraprism lens. This allows for a 6.5x optical zoom camera sensor without changing the structural thickness of the device. Coupled with upgraded 48MP resolution sensors on both Main and Ultra-Wide cameras, low-light details are preserved cleanly at high crop factors.
